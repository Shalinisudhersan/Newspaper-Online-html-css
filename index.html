Chandrayaan-4 Mission Announced by ISRO

India aims to bring moon soil samples back to Earth by 2027.

by: ShaliniSudhersan. Kanyakumari

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has officially announced its next ambitious lunar mission, Chandrayaan-4, which will attempt to bring moon soil and rock samples back to Earth. This will be India’s first sample-return mission and is expected to provide scientists with valuable insights into the moon’s composition and origin. The project highlights India’s growing leadership in space exploration.

According to ISRO scientists, Chandrayaan-4 will use advanced robotic systems capable of drilling, collecting, and safely storing lunar material for return. The mission is planned in collaboration with several international space agencies, ensuring access to shared expertise and cutting-edge technology. If successful, India will join a select group of nations that have achieved sample-return missions.

The announcement has generated excitement among citizens, students, and researchers across the country. Prime Minister praised ISRO’s relentless efforts, calling it a symbol of India’s scientific excellence. Space enthusiasts believe this mission will inspire the next generation of young scientists while placing India firmly on the global space exploration map.

India Wins Cricket World Cup 2025

Historic victory against Australia brings the trophy home after 12 years.

In a thrilling final match at the Narendra Modi Stadium in Ahmedabad, India defeated Australia to win the 2025 ICC Cricket World Cup. The stadium was packed with over one lakh cheering fans as India chased down a challenging target with style. The win marks India’s third World Cup title and the first since 2011, ending a 12-year-long wait for millions of cricket lovers.

Star batsman Virat Kohli stole the show with a stunning century, anchoring the innings with confidence and calm under pressure. The bowlers, led by Jasprit Bumrah and Mohammed Siraj, restricted Australia’s batting lineup effectively, turning the game in India’s favor. The team’s all-round performance was hailed as one of the finest displays of modern cricket.

Celebrations erupted across the nation the moment the final wicket fell. From fireworks in Delhi and Mumbai to dancing in the streets of Chennai and Kolkata, fans expressed their joy with unmatched enthusiasm. The players dedicated the victory to their supporters, while the BCCI announced a grand victory parade in Mumbai to honor the champions.

Heavy Rains Flood Major Indian Cities

Traffic chaos and rescue operations as monsoon intensifies

Several major Indian cities, including Delhi, Mumbai, and Chennai, were brought to a standstill today after continuous heavy rainfall caused severe flooding and waterlogging. Roads turned into streams, public transport faced major delays, and thousands of commuters were stranded for hours. The Meteorological Department has issued a red alert in many regions, warning of more downpours in the coming days.

Emergency services and rescue teams were deployed to evacuate residents from low-lying areas. Schools and offices were instructed to remain closed, while flights and trains were canceled in several cities. Authorities are working to pump out water from critical areas, but the intensity of the rain has made relief operations challenging. Farmers, however, welcomed the rainfall as a blessing for their crops.

On social media, citizens shared shocking visuals of submerged roads, floating vehicles, and people wading through waist-deep water. Environmentalists pointed out that poor drainage systems and unplanned urbanization worsened the crisis. Officials have urged people to stay indoors and assured that measures are being taken to prevent further disruption.
